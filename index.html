Ivan Franko

Chronological table

Year Event
August 27, 1856 Ivan Franko was born in the village of Nahuyevychi, Drohobych district, Eastern Galicia (Lviv region), in the family of a blacksmith.
1862-1864 Students at the primary school in Yasenytsia-Silna village.
1864-1867 Studying at a school at the Basilian monastery in Drohobych.
1871 Franko wrote his first poem about the death of his father.
1874 The poem "Folk Song" was published in the Lviv magazine "Druh".
1867-1875 Studying at the Drohobych gymnasium.
1875 Student of the Faculty of Philosophy at Lviv University, member of the editorial board of the Druh magazine
1877 Ivan Franko arrested (for 9 months)
1878 Together with Mykhailo Pavlyk, he founded the journal Public Friend, which was published under the titles Dzvin and Molot after the confiscation.
1880 Franko is arrested for the second time, accused of inciting peasants against the government. After three months in prison, Franco was under police surveillance and was forced to stop studying at the university. "Eternal Revolutionary", "It's not time...".
1881 Franko became a co-publisher of the magazine Svit. The stories "Boa constrictor" and "Boryslav laughs".
1882 He worked in the editorial office of the Zorya magazine. The story "Zakhar Berkut".
1883-1885 He worked for the Dilo newspaper.
1886 He married Olga Khoruzhynska.
1887-1897 He was an employee of the Polish newspaper Kurjer Lwowski.
1889 His ties to the Naddniprians led to his third arrest.
1890 With the support of Mykhailo Drahomanov, Franko co-founded the Russian-Ukrainian Radical Party and prepared a program for it
1891 Franko is completing his studies at the University of Chernivtsi.
1893 Social and psychological drama Stolen Happiness.
1890-1895 Together with Mykhailo Pavlyk, he published the semi-monthly "Narod" (People).
1896, 1897 Collection "Faded Leaves". Collection "My Izmaraagd".
1899 Franco, together with the People's Party, founded the National Democratic Party.
1900 Collection "From the Days of Sorrow"
1904 He stopped taking an active part in political life.
1905, 1906 The poem "Moses". The program collection "Semper tiro"
November 26, 1915. Ukrainian community in Vienna nominates Ivan Franko for the Nobel Prize (death prevented him from receiving it)
May 28, 1916 Ivan Franko died and is buried at the Lychakiv Cemetery in Lviv.
